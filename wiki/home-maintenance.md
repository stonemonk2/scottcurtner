#### PPE Protocol (Full Gear for Overhead Eave Spraying)
1. Disposable hazmat suit — full body coverage
2. Full face gas mask — eyes, nose, mouth, respiratory protection
3. Thin latex gloves under — added hand protection
4. Do NOT spray in wind — wait for calm conditions
5. Mix at lower end of dilution range when uncertain

#### Application Procedure
1. Mix Bifenthrin concentrate in Chapin pump sprayer (1 oz per gallon standard; 1.5 oz for eaves)
2. Don full PPE — hazmat suit, face mask, gloves
3. **Foundation perimeter first** — spray 3ft up the wall and 3ft out on the ground, continuous band all the way around the house
4. **Eaves second** — spray up into eave overhangs, sweep side to side, keep face mask sealed
5. Allow to dry completely before children or pets re-enter treated areas
6. **Cleanup** — rinse sprayer with clean water, remove and dispose of hazmat suit, wash hands and face
7. Store all equipment in shed

#### Safety Notes
- Bifenthrin is moderately toxic — not acutely dangerous at these dilutions but avoid direct skin, eye, and ingestion exposure
- Overhead spraying is the highest exposure risk — full face mask is non-negotiable for eave work
- Keep concentrate container in shed, away from children
- Do not spray near garden beds or water features if avoidable — toxic to fish and aquatic insects
